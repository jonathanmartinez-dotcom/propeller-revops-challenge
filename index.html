A Note Before We Start

I've spent my career sitting at the intersection of marketing and revenue — and the pattern I keep seeing is the same one. Demand gen teams build sophisticated models in isolation. Sales teams track what they can. Finance counts what closes. Everyone has data, nobody has context, and the attribution conversation becomes a negotiation instead of a north star.

The fix isn't a more complex model. A well-aligned, well-executed elementary framework will outperform two or three sophisticated ones running in silos every single time. What follows is how I'd build that alignment for Propeller — starting with the leak in the funnel and working backward to the system that prevents it.

Segmentation Logic

Three dimensions layered together: firmographic fit, acquisition channel, and behavioral signals. Channel determines messaging tone; ICP fit determines routing speed; behavior determines sequence acceleration.

High ICP — Priority Route

Mining, construction, or earthworks companies with 50+ employees. Highest ACV potential and fastest path to qualified opportunity. Routes directly to AE, not SDR.

  • Industry: Mining / Construction / Earthworks / Aggregates
  • Employee count: 50+ (via enrichment)
  • Title: Project Mgr, VDC, Site Engineer, Ops Director, Owner
  • Prior pages visited: 2+ before form
  • Acquisition: Organic or paid (high-intent channel)
Standard ICP — Nurture

Adjacent industries or smaller teams. Valid interest but longer sales cycles. Full 7-day sequence with heavier education before consultation push. SDR-led.

  • Industry: Agriculture / Energy / Waste / Surveying
  • Employee count: 10–49
  • First-time visitors, no prior CRM history
  • Acquisition: Direct or social
  • Job title outside economic buyer list
Low Fit — Suppress

Students, competitors, or out-of-market visitors. Suppressed from sales routing. Moved to a low-touch educational list only — protecting rep capacity and database health.

  • Email domain: .edu, known competitor
  • Company size: <5 employees or blank
  • Title: Student / Intern / Researcher
  • Country: outside core market regions
  • Acquisition: Direct-to-form with no prior engagement

Exit Criteria

Exit rules are segment-specific. High ICP accounts get more aggressive re-engagement before suppression. Standard ICP exits faster to protect database health.

✓ Meeting Booked (All Segments)

Contact books a consultation. All remaining emails suppress immediately. Lifecycle advances to SQL. Contact enters the consultation-to-opportunity workflow. Score preserved for attribution reporting.

✓ Rep Advances to Opportunity (All Segments)

Sales rep manually creates an opportunity or moves contact to SQL. Workflow detects lifecycle change and exits. Prevents over-communication during active sales conversations.

✗ Unsubscribe — Immediate (All Segments)

Contact unsubscribes from any step. Workflow exits. Rep notified to switch to direct outreach only. Contact flagged for suppression audit. Rep cannot re-enroll without RevOps approval.

✗ High ICP — No Engagement at Day 7

High ICP contact with zero opens, clicks, or meeting after full sequence. Do not suppress immediately. Move to a 14-day "last attempt" AE phone sequence — too high value to abandon without a direct call attempt. Suppress only after that sequence completes without response.

✗ Standard ICP — No Engagement at Day 7

Standard ICP contact with zero engagement after Day 7. Move directly to 90-day re-engagement list. Remove from active sales routing. Score reset to baseline to prevent stale data from inflating pipeline projections.

✓ Re-Engagement from 90-Day List

Contact opens an email or visits the demo page again after suppression. Re-enroll in workflow from Day 0. Score starts fresh but acquisition channel and industry data are preserved. Rep notified of re-engagement as a warm inbound signal.

Data & Measurement Plan

A dashboard built around proving impact — five metrics, one red flag, a clear before/after story, and an attribution model upgrade path from last-touch to W-shaped.

Attribution Model Context Propeller currently runs last-touch attribution. That model gives 100% credit to the final interaction before a deal closes — which systematically undercounts top-of-funnel investments like content, paid search, and community events that warm leads before they ever hit the demo form. Moving to a W-shaped model is the right next step: it preserves the clarity of milestone weighting (first touch, MQL creation, opportunity creation) without requiring the full data infrastructure of a data-driven or algorithmic model. This is not a massive jump — it is a purposeful progression. The goal is accuracy, not complexity for its own sake.

Red Flag Metric — Pause & Optimize Trigger

Unsubscribe Rate Exceeds 2% on Any Single Email Step

If any email in the sequence crosses 2% unsubscribes, pause that step and audit before re-activating. An unsubscribe spike means misalignment between message and audience — wrong timing, wrong content, or contacts who were never genuinely interested. Losing the ability to email a prospect is worse than sending one fewer nurture email. Review daily in the first 30 days post-launch, then weekly once stable.

Navattic / Arcade
Interactive Demo Tool

A completed interactive demo is the strongest pre-consultation buying signal available. It means the prospect is self-educating — not just curious. This event should skip standard nurture and trigger immediate sales action.

Navattic fires webhook on demo_completed event with email, demo name, % completed, and time spent
HubSpot custom behavioral event receives payload via Operations Hub webhook listener
Workflow branch: if demo_completed = true AND score ≥ 50 → skip Day 1 email, trigger immediate AE call task
Score incremented +20pts; Slack alert includes "completed interactive demo" flag; decay timer starts at T+48hrs
Mixpanel / Amplitude
Product Analytics

Prior product activity (trials, feature usage) is invisible to the form-based workflow without a product analytics bridge. Connecting this closes the PLG loop and enables routing based on demonstrated product engagement, not just declared intent.

Mixpanel Cohort sync or webhook pushes product_activity events to HubSpot via Operations Hub
Custom contact property "Product Sessions (Last 30 Days)" populated and scored
Workflow branch: sessions ≥ 3 → route to expansion AE, not SDR; flag as PQL not just MQL

Mutiny
Website Personalization

The demo page should adapt to who's visiting. A mining company seeing construction use cases is leaving buying signal and conversion on the table before the form even appears.

Mutiny personalizes hero copy, case study callout, and CTA by industry/company size from IP lookup

Demo Request Fast-Track

When someone clicks a "Request a Demo" CTA in a marketing email: lifecycle updates to MQL, Slack fires to #demand-gen-wins, and a rep task is created within 24 hours. Here's exactly how to build it.

01

Workflow Trigger — CTA Click in Email

Create a Contact-based HubSpot workflow. Enrollment trigger: "Email link clicked" where the URL contains your demo request CTA path (e.g. /request-a-demo). Use a URL contains filter rather than exact match to capture UTM variants. Re-enrollment enabled so repeat clicks from future campaigns re-trigger the flow.

Trigger: Email marketing → Link clicked
Filter: URL contains "/request-a-demo"
Re-enrollment: ON
Suppress: Lifecycle Stage = Customer OR already has MQL date < 24hrs
02

Update Lifecycle Stage to MQL

Set the contact's HubSpot lifecycle stage to "Marketing Qualified Lead." Use a condition first: if lifecycle is already SQL, Opportunity, or Customer, skip this step — never downgrade a lifecycle stage. Only upgrade.

Action: Set contact property
Condition: IF Lifecycle Stage is Lead OR Subscriber
THEN set Lifecycle Stage = MQL
Also set: MQL Date = Today (custom date property)
03

Slack Notification — #demand-gen-wins

Use HubSpot's native Slack integration (Operations Hub) — no Zapier needed. Post to #demand-gen-wins with contact name, company, and the specific email they clicked from. The email name field is key — it tells the team which campaign drove the click so they have context before calling.

Channel: #demand-gen-wins
Message: "🎯 MQL from email click:
{{contact.firstname}} {{contact.lastname}}
Company: {{company.name}}
Email: {{enrollment.email_name}}
View contact: {{contact.hubspot_link}}"
04

Create Follow-Up Task for Assigned Rep

Create a HubSpot task on the contact record assigned to the contact owner. Due date: 24 hours from enrollment. Task title includes the contact name and email source for instant context. If contact has no owner, assign to a default SDR rotation queue and post an additional Slack alert to #ops-unassigned.

Action: Create task
Title: "Follow up — {{contact.firstname}} clicked demo CTA in {{enrollment.email_name}}"
Due date: Today + 1 day
Assigned to: Contact owner
IF owner unknown: assign to default SDR queue

If this contact hasn't already submitted the full demo form, immediately enroll them in the Part 1 workflow at Day 0 (confirmation + calendar link). The CTA click is a softer signal than a form fill — they need to be guided to the next step, not left to find it themselves.

Edge Cases & Failure Points
Contact clicks CTA multiple times
Re-enrollment is on but suppression rule prevents MQL re-stamp within 24 hours. Slack will post for each click (acceptable visibility). Task creation uses "don't create if open task exists" condition to avoid duplicates.
Contact has no assigned owner
Branch to default SDR queue assignment. Additional Slack alert fires to #ops-unassigned with a flag. No contact should fall through without a human owner within 2 hours.
Contact is already SQL or Opportunity
Lifecycle update is suppressed — never downgrade a stage. Slack still fires (rep should know about the click) but task is not created since an active deal should already have rep ownership.
Slack integration disconnects
HubSpot logs the workflow error. Set up a HubSpot notification to the RevOps owner when workflow errors exceed 3 in 24 hours. Rep still receives the CRM task — Slack is a bonus layer, not the only alert.
Email send via external tool (not HubSpot)
If emails are sent via Outreach or a parallel tool, the HubSpot trigger won't fire. Ensure CTA links include UTM parameters that can be captured via HubSpot tracking code on the landing page as a fallback trigger.
